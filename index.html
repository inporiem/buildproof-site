What exists, what's missing

The industry already has software. Just not where it counts.

Top-of-funnel software exists. Zutec, Asite, and similar platforms manage asset registers, digital handover, and compliance reporting. They're built for the developer's records — what gets handed over once a building is finished.

Mid-funnel software exists. SiteAuditPro, Procore, and similar tools cover project management and audit packs. They're built for the office side of construction — coordinating, reporting, signing off.

Nothing exists at the bottom. The crucial moment — what was actually built, by whom, against which drawing, with which product — happens at the work face. Today that data lives on phones, in WhatsApp, in someone's head. Then it tries to flow up to the systems that were meant to receive it.

BuildProof is the bottom of the funnel. Capture at source. Everything downstream depends on it.

Where this matters most

Already required. Increasingly required.

The regulations driving change
2022 · Building Safety Act
Duty holders must retain a Golden Thread of structured, traceable records.

Personal liability on developers and building owners. Records held for the lifetime of higher-risk buildings.

2025 · Awaab's Law
Social landlords must investigate and resolve hazards within strict statutory timeframes — and document every step.

Damp and mould today. Excess cold, falls, structural collapse, fire, and electrical hazards from 2026. Records of every contractor visit, every action, every outcome are mandated by law.

2015 · CDM Regulations
Records of competence, decisions, and changes throughout a project.

The duty-holder framework that underpins every UK construction site. Evidence is the only thing that demonstrates compliance.

Extended 2022 · Defective Premises Act
